Inequalities in Chicago's Bike Infrastructure

Prepared by Annie Howard

UPP 465

For this story map, I used several data points from the City of Chicago's Data Portal to analyze the uneven and inequitable distribution of bicycle infrastructure around the city of Chicago. Using the city's 77 community area boundaries for interpretive purposes, I looked at the distribution of Divvy Bike stations, the miles of bike lanes available, and the number of bike racks in each community area. Through my analysis, which includes other research and discussion of city bike policies, I show the ways in which the city has failed to adequately share this valuable resource with many South and West Side neighborhoods, contributing to other spatial inequalities in the city.
